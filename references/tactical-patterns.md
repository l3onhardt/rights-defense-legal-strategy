# Tactical Patterns

Use these patterns to build lawful countermeasures. Select based on facts, evidence, adversarial response, forum power, cost, risk, and the user's real goal.

## 1. Evidence First

Before escalation, preserve what can disappear:

- Export chat logs, emails, order records, tickets, backend screenshots, payment records, delivery records, invoices, contracts, notices, calls, account status, app pages, platform rule pages, and service records.
- Keep originals. Save hash/time metadata when useful. Avoid editing screenshots except for copies used for presentation.
- Build a chronology with dates, actors, exact words, amounts, and evidence file names.
- Confirm whether audio/video recording is lawful and usable in context before relying on it.
- Avoid public accusations before evidence, privacy, and legal wording are checked.

Evidence should answer: **what must be proven, who denies it, and which third party will care?**

## 2. Controlled Communication

Use communication to create evidence and improve leverage:

- Write calmly and specifically.
- Ask the other side to confirm facts, reasons, policies, amounts, deadlines, responsible entity, signing authority, or contractual/platform/legal basis.
- Avoid admissions, emotional abuse, threats, false allegations, broad waivers, or irreversible concessions.
- Use “请提供你方作出该处理/扣款/解除/拒退/处罚的合同、平台规则或法律依据，以及负责主体和申诉/复核路径。” when challenged by large companies, platforms, employers, landlords, or institutions.
- Set reasonable deadlines and preserve proof of delivery.

A first message is often not for persuasion; it is to force the opponent into a written position.

## 3. Pressure Ladder

Escalate step by step:

1. Private clarification and evidence preservation
2. Evidence-building written confirmation request
3. Internal complaint, supervisor, platform appeal, or customer service escalation
4. Formal notice, demand letter, or lawyer-letter response
5. Regulator, industry association, consumer/labor/market/data/financial/housing complaint when in scope
6. Mediation or arbitration where available
7. Litigation, administrative reconsideration/litigation, criminal report, preservation, or enforcement route when applicable

At each step, ask:

```text
does this create evidence?
does it increase lawful pressure?
does it reduce uncertainty?
what will the opponent likely do next?
what will the forum/decision-maker think?
what is the next move if this fails?
```

If an escalation step cannot answer these questions, it is probably emotion, not strategy.

## 4. Letter and Message Structure

For demand or response text:

1. Identify parties and matter.
2. State verified facts with dates and evidence.
3. Cite contract/platform rule/legal basis only after verification.
4. Ask the other side to disclose their basis if it is unknown.
5. State the user's narrow demand.
6. Set a deadline.
7. Reserve rights without overthreatening.
8. Provide a settlement or correction path when useful.
9. Keep tone firm, short, and usable in court, arbitration, complaint files, or platform review.

Before finalizing, run the Draft Stress Test from `adversarial-harness.md`.

## 5. Complaint Strategy

A good complaint should identify:

- correct respondent and responsible entity
- correct regulator/platform/channel and why it has scope
- specific facts and evidence
- specific violated rule or suspected violation, verified where possible
- clear request: refund, restoration, correction, investigation, penalty, mediation, written explanation, deletion, payment, performance
- attachments list
- prior communication history and proof of delivery

Do not file random complaints. Misrouted complaints waste time, reveal strategy, and may create a weak record.

A complaint is stronger when it gives the handler an easy path to accept jurisdiction and understand the remedy.

## 6. Settlement Strategy

When settlement is possible:

- Define money, time, performance, deletion/restoration, confidentiality, non-disparagement, tax/invoice, default liability, release scope, complaint/ticket/litigation withdrawal, and evidence handling.
- Avoid broad waivers that kill unknown claims unless compensation is worth it.
- Avoid admitting fault unless strategically necessary and reviewed.
- Confirm who signs for companies and whether authority is valid.
- Preserve evidence until performance is complete.

A strong settlement offer should be cheaper for the opponent than their best lawful resistance, while safer for the user than continued escalation.

## 7. Public / Reputation Pressure

Use only when lawful, factual, proportionate, and strategically necessary.

Before public posting, check:

- verified facts versus allegations
- privacy and personal information exposure
- defamation, business reputation, confidentiality, and platform rule risks
- whether public pressure improves the outcome or only vents emotion
- whether a private demand or regulator route should happen first
- whether wording would survive court/regulator review

Prefer factual process records over insults or moral condemnation.

## 8. Risk Controls

Flag these risks early:

- defamation, business reputation, privacy, or personal information violations
- illegal debt collection, threats, coercion, harassment, or extortion
- evidence contamination, forged screenshots, deleted originals, missing metadata
- missed limitation, appeal, objection, arbitration, hearing, evidence, or platform deadlines
- wrong respondent, wrong forum, missing prerequisite, or unenforceable remedy
- signing settlement, receipt, resignation, compensation confirmation, guarantee, confession, admission, or waiver under pressure
- criminal/admin route being misused for civil leverage
- opponent counterclaim, retaliation, or preemptive record-building

When risk is high, give immediate do-not-do guidance before the full plan.
